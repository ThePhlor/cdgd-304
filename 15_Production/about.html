My name is Austin Harvey. I grew up in Lowell, Massachusetts. I am currently a senior at the Massachusetts College of Art and Design where I am working to receive my Bachelors of Fine Art with a concentration in Illustration. I work in traditional media and tend to gravitate towards acrylic paint. I enjoy rendering the human face and I attempt to capture the emotion and narrative that are present in facial expression.

I have never thought of myself as an artist. Sitting at the studio desk I occupy on loan from the Massachusetts College of Art and Design, I wonder how I managed to deceive so many into believing otherwise, in pursuit of my bachelor's degree. I sketch out ideas for my next piece. I aim to tell a story. I distract my audience with my perspective on an old tale. I divert their attention from my inability to come up with the right words. I divert their attention from my inability to come up with new words. I want to tell them something I’ve heard. I value communication, but language is complicated and it divides us by the tongue we speak, the education we've received and our relative attention span. Imagery, on the other hand, tends to be omni-lingual. It is powerful and immediate. The caves of Monte Castillo in Spain contain the world’s earliest dated cave paintings. Simple hand prints dye the walls with their red outlines. Sitting dormant for over 40,000 years, they wait to tell the stories of one’s existence on this earth. For more than 40,000 years, we have hungered to project our presence, to let others know that we lived and that we have seen things worth communicating. We are infatuated with the idea of being able to transmit our own thoughts and experiences to one another. We crave to be heard and understood, no matter how sharp our minds or skilled our tongues. That is why I am not an artist. I am a journalist who majored in illustration. I am a re-teller of stories.
